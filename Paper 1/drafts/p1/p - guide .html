Paper 1 – Bimodal Joint Population Guide

From field joints to a statistically defensible Najd / Red Sea structural signal

Bimodal orientation · Circular statistics Draft for internal planning • Makkah region, Saudi Arabia

This guide outlines the structure, methods, and key messages of Paper 1, which statistically characterises co‑existing Najd (NW–SE) and Red Sea (NE–SW) joint families at five road‑cut sites using circular statistics and a simple bimodal classification. [file:64][file:65][web:82]

Section 3 Data and methods – circular and bimodal analysis
3
  • Data:
    • Five sites, ~100 joint (or slope) dip‑direction measurements per site; dip angles measured. [file:64][file:65]
    • Standard compass‑clinometer methods and recording in 0–360° azimuth.
  • Circular statistics:
    • Compute mean dip direction, resultant length \(R\), and circular variance for each site. [file:64][web:82][web:89]
    • Apply Rayleigh test to assess non‑uniformity (uniform vs clustered). [file:64][web:82]
  • Bimodal classification:
    • Define Najd windows (e.g., 310–340° and 130–160° rotated by 180°). [file:64][file:65]
    • Define Red Sea windows (e.g., 40–70° and 220–250°). [file:64][file:65]
    • Assign each measurement:
      • Najd if within Najd window(s).
      • Red Sea if within Red Sea window(s).
      • Other otherwise. [file:64][file:65]
  • Visualisation: rose diagrams and mean‑vector compass plots generated for each site. [file:64][web:70]

Section 5 Discussion – structural interpretation and implications
5
  • Structural interpretation:
    • Joint sets align with both Najd (NW–SE) and Red Sea (NE–SW) trends, consistent with regional models of Red Sea reactivation of Najd fabrics. [file:65][web:42][web:44][web:53]
    • Differences between sites (Najd‑dominated, Red Sea‑dominated, mixed) reflect local variations in reactivation, overprinting, and proximity to major shear/fault zones. [file:64][file:65][web:47][web:58]
  • “Other” population:
    • 30–60% of joints fall outside strict Najd/Red Sea windows; likely represent other Precambrian shear systems, bedding/foliation trends, and geomorphic controls. [file:64][file:65][web:58][web:61][web:49]
  • Engineering implications:
    • Confirms that rock slopes at these road cuts are structurally controlled, not random. [file:64][web:70]
    • Provides the quantitative foundation for the Najd Intensity Index and design‑oriented work in subsequent papers. [file:65][web:87]
Section 6 Conclusions – concise messages
6
  • Joint orientations at four out of five Najd‑affected road‑cut sites are significantly clustered and bimodal. [file:64]
  • Najd and Red Sea joint families co‑exist at all sites with site‑specific proportions. [file:64][file:65]
  • A substantial residual “Other” category highlights additional structural and geomorphic controls, motivating broader structural classification in future work. [file:64][file:65][web:58][web:61]
